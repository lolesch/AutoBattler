**High value — design not yet locked:**

- **Status Effects** — each one individually (Burn, Freeze, Shock, Poison). They have terrain interactions, stacking rules, and counter-plays that fit every field cleanly. The template would force you to define _opposition_ and _polarity_ per effect, which seems mostly unresolved.
- **Resource system** (Mana vs Health as separate entries, then the overflow → shield mechanic as a third). Risk/Reward and Polarity fields would pressure-test the overflow idea quickly.
- **Two-phase Combat Model** — Phase 1 (Positioning) and Phase 2 (Resolution) as separate entries. The _Opposition_ and _Polarity_ fields alone would surface whether the two phases have genuine tension or just sequencing.

**Medium value — design mostly stable but gaps visible:**

- **PawnEffect / Aura** — purpose and depth are clear; _opposition_ and _polarity_ are not designed yet (what counters an aura? how do you disable it?)
- **Terrain system** — _progress_ field (why invest into terrain interaction?) is the key gap
- **Build archetypes** (Tank, Burst, Glass Cannon) — filling in _Opposition_ and _Polarity_ per archetype would be a direct enemy design brief

**Lower priority — mostly resolved:**

- **Inventory spatial design** — the template fits but most answers are already implicit in Backpack Battles-style precedents
- **Hex positioning as a mechanic** — probably better handled as part of the Combat or PawnEffect entries
